locked myself out of my flat:

This is an issue that most of us have encountered.. and it is like any problem with variables and may have several possible functions to return a solutions. It starts with confusion..maybe shaking something.. talking to yourself, or a rubber duck (which you may try to use as a key at some point).
After lots of time we came up with an option: 

function getKeyAction(key) { 
          const keys = { 
            "break door": "Wrong answer", 
            "call mum": "Wrong answer", 
            "throw rocks at flatmates window": "Wrong answer", 
            "sleep on porch like a cat": "Wrong answer", 
            "climb into second floor window": "Yup! Don't forget your key dummy!" 
            }; 
            return keys[key] || "Wrong answer"; 
          } 
          Example usage:
          console.log(getKeyAction("break door")); // Output: Wrong answer
          console.log(getKeyAction("climb into second floor window")); // Output:
          Yup! Don't forget your key dummy!

elegantly solved a problem.

Most of the people who know me wouldn't consider me an elegant problem-solver by default. I have de-escalated a situation on a hike over the Tongariro crossing. One of friend-of-a-friend hadn't hiked before and struggled through the first 30 minutes into the track. The other people was getting annoyed and tensions (and safety) were starting to be of concern. I suggested that the others carry on with the climb, and I stayed with this person while she rested. It wasn't an issue for me and I could tell that she was a little disappointed in herself, and we couldn't safely finish the tramp. I think where this became an elegant solution was where I suggested we take our time and walk to the base of the mountain, and for her to walk maybe a few meters onto the mountain. A 1 hour trip took 4, but we learned lots about each other, and had an opportunity to appreciate and discuss the flora in the area. She also had lots of pics

Reflect on a time you were reluctant to ask for help. Consider what made you reluctant to do so. What might you try differently next time?

When I was a student in high school, there were expectations from teachers, and the school, to represent 'us' at academic competitions. I intepreted this as, "don't ask questions, because we expect you to know this".
This likely wasn't the complete story, but I did hit the wall and almost failed one of the years. The funny thing was that teachers and my folks were not aware I struggled with it.
What I have changed over time are the way I think, and what I want to do with my time:

  • We can't be smart at everything, and you can always learn from others if you ask questions.
  • It is more fun to learn something by choice, instead of wasting your time on something you have to learn.
